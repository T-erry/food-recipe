Pilau

About the food

Pilau is a popular East African rice dish (especially in Kenya and Tanzania), made by cooking rice with beef, chicken, or vegetables, in broth and spices. It goes by many names such as pilaf (US) and pilau (UK). The earliest documented recipe goes back to the 20th Century scholar Abu Ali Sina.

    Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon pilau masala
  • 2 cups of rice

    • Instructions:

    • STEP 1: ADD THE ONIONS AND WHOLE SPICES INTO THE PAN AND COOK THE UNTIL ONIONS ARE TRANSLUCENT.
    • STEP 2: ADD THE OTHER SPICES AND TOAST FOR 15 MINUTES ON LOW
    • STEP 3: SHORTLY AFTER, ADD THE BEEF AND COOK DOWN UNTIL BROWNED SLIGHTLY.
    • STEP 4: ADD THE TOMATO PASTE AND ALLOW TO BROWN.
    • STEP 5: ADD GARLIC AND GINGER PASTE AND COOK THROUGH UNTIL BROWNED, TOSS FOR 10 MINUTES BEFORE ADDING THE WASHED RICE.
    • STEP 6: ADD 5 CUPS OF BOILING WATER AND COVER THE RICE AND COOK IT UNTIL ALL WATER HAS EVAPORATED AND RICE HAS COOKED THROUGH.
